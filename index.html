I think you so look deeper

There was a time when I believed that the world was simple. That people said what they meant. That smiles were real. That silence meant peace. But life has a quiet way of teaching you things that no book ever could. It started with a conversation I didn’t expect to have. A small moment, with someone I barely noticed before, ended up changing how I saw myself and the world. I was sitting alone in a quiet café, staring at my phone, endlessly scrolling through other people’s perfect lives. Perfect vacations. Perfect skin. Perfect relationships. Perfect happiness. And there I was — stuck in the same place, feeling like I was falling behind some invisible race that everyone else understood except me. That’s when the stranger spoke. “You keep looking out,” they said softly. “But I think you should look deep.” At first, I laughed. It sounded like something from a movie — dramatic and unnecessary. But their eyes were calm, steady, and honest. They weren’t judging me. They weren’t trying to impress me. They were just… real. So I did. That night, for the first time in years, I switched off my phone and sat alone with my thoughts. No music. No distractions. No pretending. Just me. And it was terrifying. I realized how much pain I had buried under jokes. How many dreams I had called “unrealistic” just to avoid failure. How often I had chosen comfort over growth. How many times I told myself “I’m fine” when I wasn’t. Looking deep wasn’t about finding darkness. It was about finding truth. I found a scared version of myself. A hopeful version. A tired version. A version that still believed in magic, even after being let down too many times. I began to understand that healing doesn’t look beautiful. It looks like crying in the shower. It looks like staring at the ceiling at 3 a.m. It looks like learning to forgive yourself for things you did when you were just trying to survive. The more I looked deep, the more I realized that I wasn’t broken. I was human. And maybe that’s the part no one talks about enough. We’re all walking around with invisible stories, invisible scars, invisible battles. Everyone you meet is carrying something heavy. Smiles can lie. Silence can scream. Now when I feel lost, I don’t try to escape it. I sit with it. I listen. Because somewhere along the way, I learned that growth doesn’t come from pretending everything is okay. It comes from honesty. From courage. From going into the places inside yourself that you were once afraid to visit. So if you ever feel lost, empty, confused, or tired of pretending… I think you should look deep. Not for perfection. Not for answers. But for yourself.
